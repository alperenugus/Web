Course Selection

A new student who will begin his/her undergraduate program is automatically enrolled to the courses by the registrar's office once. For other terms, the student is expected to prepare his/her own program. Every course has a code called CRN and before course selections, the classes which will be available, their schedules and quota will be announced in ITU Registrar's Office. When the enrollment time comes, the students enter the CRN codes of the courses in their programs in the page 'Student Service/Registration/Add/Drop Classes', and if they provide the prerequisites of the courses and if the courses have enough quota, the students will be enrolled to the courses by the system. Do not forget that the system clock differs from the real one by 1,5-2 minutes. Easy to guess, there are averagely 7-8 CRN codes and entering them by hand takes a lot of time. For this purpose, İTÜ-Scheduler is created by ITU students. In this process, to learn about the courses you have to pass or if they have prerequisites and time schedule, you should go to the links below and look for them:

Internship

In order to be able to graduate from ITU Computer Engineering, it is compulsory to be an intern for 40 work-days that consists of two internship programs of 20 work-days. The university suggests students to do internship in the summers of second and third year. According to my experience, a first year student just takes the pool courses and one or two departmentental courses therefore the student does not have enough knowledge to be a part of a real project. Moreover, companies prefer third and fourth year students to continue with them after graduation. In my opinion, an undergraduate student should do internship by converting his/her theoretical knowledge into practical one. My own internship changed my whole perspective to the department and being a part of a real project refreshed my self-esteem.
Anything about the internship is on ITU-Portal. I suggest you to read internship guidelines carefully.
